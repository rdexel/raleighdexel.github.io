About Me.

Hello, my name is Raleigh Dexe! I’m a college studying business with hopes to someday create a disruptive company that positively affects millions of people. My entrepreneurial spirit has been part of my identity for as long as I can remember, and it all started when I was in 5th selling novelty cards at local craft fairs. I would create small watercolor paintings using my artistic abilities, copy those paintings, and manually place them on small cards. While they were tedious to make, I always enjoyed the process and I sold out almost every time. As my artistic interest transitioned to photography, I became obsessed with drones and aerial photography. After saving up my money and purchasing my first drone, I got in touch with some local real estate agents. This led to my second business, Dexel Aerial Photography, which allowed me to offer my services as an aerial property photographer and build connections that will last a lifetime. Over the past couple of years, I have become increasingly interested in technology, computers, and the web. Using my web design skills, I was able to help multiple small businesses in my area by creating beautiful websites and expanding their digital presence. While I started using website design programs like Wix, my interest in computers has pushed me towards learning languages like HTML, CSS to build custom websites like this one. I have also started learning the Python programming language to further challenge myself and develop foundational knowledge regarding application development. As I continue to develop my programming skills, study business, and expand my personal network, I am confident that I will be able to create a startup with the ability to positively impact the lives of many. While my business ambitions are clear, It is also important to discuss my personal past. I’m currently a 17-year-old based in Olympia, WA. During my early life, I was homeschooled rather than being sent to a public elementary school. Due to the flexibility that the homeschool curriculum offered, I was able to skip two grades before entering 7th grade at a public middle school at the age of 11. While I continued to excel academically throughout my middle school experience, the age gap between me and my peers made me feel slightly isolated. However, as I progressed through high school, I learned that the differences between me and my peers helped me learn about individualism. Functioning independently helped me build my confidence and embrace my young age. After the COVID-19 pandemic forced schools to start teaching online, I decided to switch to a running start program at South Puget Sound Community College for my senior year. I graduated with the class of 2021 at the age of 16, which has led me to where I am now: a 17-year-old Sophomore at South Puget Sound Community College. This year, I plan to earn my Associates in Business before transferring to a four-year university. If you’ve read this far, thank you! I appreciate your interest in my personal journey and I encourage you to explore other parts of the website or get in touch with me!
