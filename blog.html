08/30/21: Explaining my investment in Hedera Hashgraph

On my way home from a property photoshoot the other day, I decided to listen to a podcast called Crypto 101. I’ve been skeptical of crypto for the past couple of months, mostly because of the volatility and the worthless altcoins appearing on the market. But for some reason, my curiosity led me to listen to an interview with the founder of Hedera Hashgraph, Leemon Baird. Hedera Hashgraph is a new type of distributed ledger technology, which is basically a computer that exists among tons of different nodes on tons of different computers. The Hedera Hashgraph network, rather than being based on traditional blockchain technology, is based on a new type of consensus algorithm. It relies on nodes that gossip to each other and eventually come to a collective consensus about the validity and history of a transaction. While I won’t go super far into the details, this technology is absolutely revolutionary in terms of crypto. The Hashgraph network can handle upwards of 10,000 transactions per second with a $0.01 transaction fee. Considering modern blockchain technologies like those employed by Bitcoin and Ethereum offer slow tps rates and astronomical gas fees, Hedera Hashgraph is on a completely different level. Due to the multiple corporations involved in the governance of Hedera Hashgraph (Google, IBM, Boeing, LG, Tata Communications, etc.), some argue that the Hedera network is not classified as a decentralized asset. However, this is simply irrelevant; Hedera’s goals have never been aligned with being a completely decentralized currency. Rather, Hedera aims to be a public utility that can be used by anybody. While it will take some time for people to accept that Hedera is not a typical DeFi currency, Hedera Hashgraph’s involvement with large companies and the sheer amount of projects happening on the network prove that it is on it’s way to mass adoption.
